Nand Kishore Chaudhary


Weaver.Thinker.Doer

Mr. Nand Kishore Chaudhary (NKC), the founder of Jaipur Rugs, is a globally acclaimed social entrepreneur who is often referred to as the ‘Gandhi of carpet industry’.

His journey began in 1978 when he started with two looms and nine artisans. Four decades later, Jaipur Rugs has become a global social enterprise that provides sustainable livelihood to 40,000 artisans. Of these artisans over 80% are women, who work out of the comfort of their homes in 600 remote villages across five states in India. He has created immense value in society by using business as an agent of progression and change.

Under his leadership Jaipur Rugs has won several global and domestic awards like IFF Design Award, 2019, Green Parrot Award 2019, German Design Award Gold 2018 and European Product Design Award – to name a few.

Management guru, C.K. Prahalad featured NKC’s business philosophy in his globally acclaimed book – The Fortune at the Bottom of the Pyramid.

He was described as the ‘father of modern social enterprises’ by internationally reputed professor of marketing, Jagdish N. Sheth from Emory University, USA. Sheth lauded Jaipur Rugs for being an example of how a business can serve society and be a capitalistic institution at the same time.
